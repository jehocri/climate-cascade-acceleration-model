Physical Mechanism: Accelerating Feedbacks

Thermal Ocean Coupling & Ice Sheet Instability

When marine heatwaves push sea surface temperatures to unprecedented highs, they trigger a profound domino effect. Warm ocean waters erode the buttressing ice shelves of **Greenland** and **West Antarctica** from below. Removing these marine "brakes" causes land-based glaciers to slide into the ocean at an exponential rate. This massive influx of freshwater disrupts thermohaline circulation (AMOC) and decreases global albedo, locking the ocean into a compounding warming cycle.
